Purpose
In this study, we want to examine so-called phishing attacks. Phishing is the attempt to obtain sensitive pieces of information. For example, you receive an e-mail stating that you have to log in to your Amazon account to prevent it from being permanently deleted. The e-mail contains a link to a website that looks similar to the actual Amazon login website. However, when you enter your login details on this website, you do not log into your Amazon account, but your credentials get stolen.

One way to identify such fake websites is by their domain. A domain is the "name" of a website that tells your Internet browser where to go, e.g., www.amazon.com takes you to Amazon's American website. Cyber criminals often use similar domains (e.g., www.amazen.com) to lure victims on websites under the cyber criminals' control and steal the victim's credentials. This survey aims at understanding how trustworthy humans rate fake domains. We will also ask you to create fake domains to understand how humans create these. Note that all created domains are only used in the context of this scientific study and are not used in real-world phishing attacks.
